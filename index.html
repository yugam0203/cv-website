About Me

I am a postgraduate researcher with Master in Computer Science at the University of Jammu, where my work bridges machine learning, explainable AI, and psychological data modeling. My current research proposes the Hybrid SVM–XGBoost Geometric–Gradient Architecture (SXGA), an interpretable multi-domain framework that integrates statistical learning with transparent model reasoning. I am deeply interested in advancing technically grounded and interpretable ML systems that unify predictive performance with scientific insight—particularly in applications that demand trust, causality, and human-aligned understanding. My broader goal is to pursue doctoral research at the intersection of machine learning theory, hybrid modeling, and explainability, contributing to both algorithmic development and cross-domain translation of AI methods in academia and research labs.

Ongoing / In-Progress Work

Extending SXGA: Hybrid Interpretability & Cross-Domain Generalization

Status: Ongoing — extension & expert validation

More Information (click to expand)

Building upon the original hybrid architecture, this ongoing study focuses on expanding interpretability and generalization across psychological and behavioral datasets. The project aims to strengthen cross-domain robustness, integrate clinician-oriented explanation dashboards, and introduce constrained counterfactual reasoning for actionable insight generation. We are also developing evaluation frameworks to quantify interpretive consistency between machine explanations and expert judgments.

  • Developing an interactive Force SHAP visualization platform for clinician feedback and comparative model interpretation.
  • Introducing structured counterfactual constraints to ensure realistic and psychologically valid feature perturbations.
  • Evaluating cross-dataset generalization with demographic and behavioral stratification to improve fairness and model adaptability.
  • Conducting expert-in-the-loop validation to bridge model reasoning with theoretical and clinical insights.

This extension aims to transform the hybrid pipeline into a scalable, explainable, and human-aligned ML framework that can adapt across domains — from mental health analytics to broader AI applications requiring interpretability, trust, and real-world decision support.

Research Projects

Predictive Model for Detection of Psychological Distress Using Machine Learning and Psychometric Analysis

Original Dataset Thesis · Demo

Hybrid ML pipeline (SVM + XGBoost) trained on a self-collected psychological dataset integrating DASS-21, SWLS, and BFI-10 measures to predict affective states with explainable reasoning.

Role: Lead researcher — designed survey instruments, recruited participants, curated dataset, developed hybrid models, and evaluated interpretability.

Data Collection: Personally collected and anonymized data from participants via standardized psychological questionnaires ensuring ethical consent and balanced representation.

  • Approach: Feature engineering on psychometric scales, hybrid SVM+XGBoost architecture, and multi-level XAI pipeline.
  • Key result: Strong predictive performance and interpretable insights (detailed metrics in thesis).
  • Impact: Deployed prototype (PsychoPredictor AI) for real-time screening and clinician review.

Smart Gas Sensor Interface — Real-Time Data Acquisition & ML Prediction Platform Active
